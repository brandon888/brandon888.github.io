Period 8

1 Red Hill Road, Hong Kong

I chose to draw from this angle mainly because of the Chinese character. I often write in Chinese, but it’s not very often that I draw one in 3D, especially one as complicated as the 双喜. This character is actually not a real character, but two 喜 next to each other, which means double happiness. I thought the meaning behind the character and its aesthetic symmetry would create a sense of sense of unity in the drawing. In addition, I chose to draw from an angle where items would overlap to emphasize depth in the image.

The focal point of my piece is the center, where the right side of the Chinese character is. This is because the two edges of the block serve as natural leading lines that guide the eyes toward the center. In addition, the angle of the Chinese character makes it seem like the edges are convening towards the center. To enhance the focus, I also, shaded in part of the Chinese character to create negative space and let it stand out more. I used several methods to make my objects 3-dimension
