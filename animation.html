1 Red Hill Road, Hong Kong

1.Planning

I created a rough draft of important scenes for the animation. It’s a relatively simple storyline but the plot is clear. What I had in mind was one of those short, cute, emotion-provoking animations usually about animals that Pixar makes. Obviously I don’t have the animating experience, but that is what I started brainstorming with.

2: Creating the Characters and Setting

My first lesson was devoted to just creating the characters and settings. I used Procreate to create these, because it is an application I am very familiar and comfortable with using. Most animation apps do not provide the ease of use and complexity in drawing, so the layers and brushes of procreate made it easier.

3: The Actual Animation

I chose to use Adobe Animate 2019 because I used it once a long time ago, and it provides many features like masking, layering, tweening, and keyframes to make smooth animations more easily.

In the second lesson I mainly added my Procreate drawings into the application and spent time getting to understand how Adobe Animate worked.

The image above shows one of my favorite features of Adobe Animate. Double clicking an object allows you to give the object a sort of “subanimation”. Here I created the snowman walking animation (body sways left and right). This animation constantly loops while I move the snowman around in my animation, so I don’t have to worry about creating the walking animation every time.

Another feature I loved was the masking feature. In the photo below you can also see a blue circle covering the globe. This black circle represents a mask, meaning that anything on the mask layer that isn’t inside that black circle will not be seen in the end animation. I used this to create the snow in the globe
