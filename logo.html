I feel like this logo accurately represents many aspects of my life and what is most meaningful to me. As I said earlier, the brainstorming process allowed me to connect the different aspects of my life and to explore my identity. At this point in my life, I feel like the most important thing to me is following my passions and taking opportunities. In life, there are always inevitable ups and downs, but I try hard to look past the downs and remember that life is only as meaningful as you make it. That is why I love a more comical approach at art, choosing to give life to my logo and using a cheerful color scheme.

How does your logo work in terms of the Elements and Principles? Please explain (Positive and negatives space, use of colour, balance, etc) Use clear photos your of sketches and research as part of the reflection.

In striving to create a more cartoony logo that fit my personal style, I chose not to strictly follow the principles of design, but to take a slightly different approach at them. The different sized eyes and asymmetrical legs created a lack of balance and proportion, but gave the character unique features that could stand out. The straight face instead of a smiley face gave the computer an emotion of indifference that was up for interpretation. The legs, arms, and feet are oddly proportioned, as commonly seen in cartoon characters. This, to me, invokes a feeling humor, which is exactly what I wanted to bring into the logo. Also important in my logo was the use of space and color. The space between the body and the legs, again invokes this lighthearted, comical feeling, as well as the use of cheerful, vibrant colors.

2. What part of the process did you enjoy the most? The technical/digital or hand drawn aspect? What were the pros and cons of drawing by hand and on the ipad?

Using an iPad, like the previous projects, was so fun and convenient. It allowed me to portray the images in my head much more easily than with a pen or pencil. Again, the use of layers allowed me to draw freely without worrying too much about mistakes. The color detection tool allowed me to use the exact colors I wanted and I could easily drag colors into the images. Save for the therapeutic feeling of coloring on paper, this allowed me to experiment with colors much more conveniently. However, I will admit that drawing on paper allows me to have a much better grasp of line thickness compared to the perfectly smooth, glossy touch of the Apple Pencil on the iPad. This is why I prefer to write my notes on paper much more than on an iPad. Overall, I still feel like the convenience and ability to explore different color schemes and brushes on the iPad provides a much better experience for art. Do you actually like your logo?

I absolutely do. As soon as I thought of the final idea for my logo, something happened in my brain and I was happy that I created something that represented myself so well. Although the extension tasks showed that it may not be the best for marketing purposes, I feel I achieved my purpose in this task.
